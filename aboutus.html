Brief Background

In 1994, the World Council of Credit Union (WOCCU) was here in the Philippines. This trade organization of the international Credit Union System, in partnership with the Freedom from Hunger (FFH) founded by the Office of the Micro-Enterprise of the United States Agency for International Development (USAID), initiated a project in the Philippines known as Credit Union Empowerment and Strengthening (CUES) Philippines. The project was based in Mindanao, particularly in Davao City.

CUES Philippines reformed the landcape of credit cooperatives following ist goals to improve the performance of the credit cooperatives and to provide financial services to the segments of the population who cannot access credit.

The project focuses on two major thrusts: Model Credit Union Building (MCUB) and Savings and Credit with Education (SCWE). The Model Credit Union Building is technoology-based that aims at stabilizing the cooperatives by providing safe and sound credit and savings services. Its major component is the PEARLS Monitoring System that performs calculations on 46 financial ratios. These cooperative partners that successfully use the PEARLS Monitoring System were awarded the FOCCUS Brand. On the other hand, the Savinbgs and credit with Education (SCWE) is a financial education service designed for low income but economically active women wo are capable of successfully engaging in micro-enterprise.

The integration of MCUB and SCWE technology to the cooperative made CUES Philippines unique from the other credit union strengthening initiatives in the country. CUES Philippines aimed not to direct partners, but to train and help become financially outstanding cooperatives by providing financial tools.

When the project was ending, CUES Philippines and its partner cooperative saw the need of contuinig the program using the same technology because of its strategic advantage and impeccable assistance to them. Thus, MODEL COOPERATIVE NETWORK (MCN) was organized.

MCN is a federation of savings and credit cooperatives in the Philippines duly registered by the Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) last September 7, 2004. It awards the use of the trademark to affiliates that have achieved and maintanied the International Prudential Standards in their operations. These standards help primary cooperativs ensure that their members' money is safe and will grow, offer cooperative and quality financial services to its members and ensure sustainability of the cooperative operations

The federation helps cooperatives achieve and maintain their FOCCUS-Brand status through strict adherence to financial Disciplines.

Consequent to the ambitious platform and blueprint, MCN continues to uphold, promote and protect the integrity of te FOCCUS Brand, MCN's primary servise is supervision and monitoring of the member cooperatives in order to ensure SAFETY and SUSTAINABILITY. The integrity of the cooperativs is monitored and assessed based on the context of Model Credit Union Building (MCUB) guidelines.

In 2004, the CUES Philippines project ended. The cooperatives that were partners of the project were left with the challenge of sustaining the gains they have achieved. The cooperatives decided to form a federation to continue the monitoring and training services that CUES Philippines provided. The federation was named Model Cooperative Network (MCN).

FOCCUS stands for Finance Organization achieving Certified Union Standards. It is owned by MCN by virtue of the Deed of Transfer from WOCCU. The use of trademark is regulated by MCN. Cooperative who fail to maintain the prudential standards set by the federation are barred from using the trademark. MCN has qualified examiners who monitor the performance of the affiliate coorperatives on a monthly basis. Thus, cooperatives leaders are made aware of the deficiences as well as the strengths in the cooperative operation and take corrective actions before they become detrimental.

Why is FOCCUS important for a healthy cooperative sectort?

Adherence to the disciplines and standards required by FOCCUS will help ensure proliferation of healthy financial organizations in the community that are able to provide quality finance services to its members. This could help in building better lives for many poor families and also fuel local economic development.

This will also boost the image of the cooperatives enabling them to reach out and serve more members. On the other hand, this will also encourage the setting up of new cooperatives that offer services other than financial in nature.

The use of FOCCUS brand has two-fold benefits for the cooperatives. FIRST, PRUDENTIAL STANDARDS and FINANCIAL DISCIPLINES can be used as a management tool that will help coopetatives build its assets, improve the quality of services, expand operations, and boost profitability thereby ensuring sustainability of operations.

The following Financial Discipline adhered to by FOCCUS cooperatives have been proven to improve cooperatives operations:

Deliquency Control

should be not more than 5% of the loan portfolio; Director, Officers, and Staff can be expelled on the grounds of loan deliquency.

Control of Non-earning Assets

should not be more that 5% of the total assets

Liquidity Management

liquid assets should not be less than 20% of its deposit liabilities and the other short-term liabilities.

Provision for Possible Loan Losses

100% for loans that are more than 12 months delinquent, 35% for loans that are delinquent for 30-360 days

Institutional Capital Accumulation

reserves should be maintained equal to 10% of the total assets

Earnings Improvement

(limit operating cost to under 5% of total assets) and (returns on assets is not lower than the inflation rate)


The Prudential Standards used by MCN is known as the PEARLS Standards. These standards are regularly monitored in a FOCCUS Cooperative together with the PESOS and PISO indicators as required by CDA for Cooperatives with Savings and Credit Operations.

In addition to PEARLS, MCN also monitors compliance with the PESOS and PISO indicators as required by the CDA for Cooperatives with Savings and Credit Operations.

Examiners of the federation will immediately report to the cooperative officers and directors any deviation to these disciplines in its Operations and failure to reach the standards by the cooperative as well as other warning signs that might endanger cooperative operations. Thus, problems are detected easily and early while corrective actions can still be implemented.

SECOND, the FOCCUS brand acts as a third-party accreditation that alerts members of the status of the operations of their cooperative. Once the FOCCUS sign is still used by the coop, members are assumed that their money is safe, is used soundly, and operations will be sustainable.
